## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are students and instrumentalists who need to quickly manage, find, and reorder sheet music during daily practice and rehearsal.
- Core job-to-be-done: reduce friction between "I need this score now" and "I can view/use it immediately" across desktop and mobile.

### Brand Personality
- 3-word personality: lively, creative, clear.
- Emotional goals: encourage practice momentum, make task flow feel light and confident, keep actions obvious under time pressure.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Direction: playful-but-practical interface with strong clarity, expressive accents, and disciplined hierarchy.
- Theme expectation: support both light and dark themes.
- Anti-direction: avoid generic enterprise blandness and avoid decorative effects that reduce readability.

### Quality Bar & Ship Expectation
- Quality bar: flagship-level polish.
- Polish strategy: preserve functional stability first, then refine interaction states, spacing rhythm, typography consistency, and responsive behavior.

### Design Principles
1. Fast first, fancy second: every visual decision must improve scanning speed and action confidence.
2. One interaction language: consistent motion, spacing scale, and state feedback across pages.
3. Playful clarity: add personality through color/shape/micro-motion without sacrificing legibility.
4. Mobile parity: critical actions remain obvious and reachable on small screens.
5. Accessibility by default: visible focus, sufficient contrast, and reduced-motion support are baseline requirements.
